🔐 Crypto Security Basics
Is crypto actually safe to own? +

Crypto itself is secured by blockchain technology — one of the most tamper-proof systems ever built. The risk doesn't come from the technology. It comes from how people store and manage their crypto. Leaving funds on an exchange, using a weak password, or not backing up a seed phrase — those are the real dangers. That's exactly what we fix.

What is a hardware wallet and do I really need one? +

A hardware wallet is a physical device — about the size of a USB drive — that stores your crypto completely offline, disconnected from the internet. If you hold any meaningful amount of crypto, yes, you need one. Exchanges can be hacked. Online accounts can be compromised. A hardware wallet ensures your assets are safe even if every online account you own is breached. We set yours up correctly as part of every package.

What is a seed phrase and why does it matter so much? +

A seed phrase is a set of 12 to 24 words that is the master key to your entire crypto wallet. Anyone who has your seed phrase has complete, permanent access to all your funds. Losing it means losing your crypto — forever, with no recovery option. Storing it correctly is the single most important thing a crypto holder can do. Your Master Key Binder solves this with a secure, documented storage system we build together.

What's the difference between storing crypto on an exchange vs. a personal wallet? +

When your crypto is on an exchange like Coinbase, you don't actually own the private keys — the exchange does. If the exchange is hacked, goes bankrupt, or freezes your account, your funds could be at risk. A personal hardware wallet gives you true ownership. You hold the keys. No company can freeze or take your assets. We help you understand both and set up the right structure for your situation.

What are the most common ways people lose their crypto? +

The most common causes of crypto loss we see:

  • Lost or improperly stored seed phrases
  • Phishing scams — fake emails or websites stealing login credentials
  • Romance and investment scams (pig butchering)
  • Sending crypto to the wrong address
  • Exchange hacks or collapses
  • Forgetting passwords or losing access to accounts
  • Family members unable to access funds after a loved one passes

Every one of these is preventable with the right setup — which is what we provide.

Can crypto transactions be reversed if I send to the wrong address? +

No. Blockchain transactions are permanent and irreversible. Once you send crypto, it cannot be recalled. This is why our Golden Transfer Protocol is so important — it gives you a clear, step-by-step checklist for every transfer scenario so you never make a costly mistake. It's included with every Master Key Binder.

🚨 Scams & Fraud Prevention
How do I know if something is a crypto scam? +

The biggest red flags to watch for:

  • Anyone promising guaranteed returns or "can't miss" investment opportunities
  • Someone you met online or on social media asking you to send crypto
  • Unsolicited messages claiming your account is at risk and asking you to act immediately
  • Requests to move your crypto to a "safer" wallet they provide
  • Anyone asking for your seed phrase, password, or private keys — ever
  • Fake customer support from exchanges or wallets contacting you first

If it feels urgent, too good to be true, or someone is pressuring you — stop and call us first.

What is a pig butchering scam? +

Pig butchering is one of the most devastating crypto scams operating today. A scammer — often posing as a romantic interest, old friend, or successful investor — builds trust over weeks or months before introducing a fake crypto investment platform. Victims are encouraged to deposit funds and shown fake profits to get them to invest more. When they try to withdraw, the platform disappears along with all their money. Florida residents have lost hundreds of thousands of dollars to this scam. Our scam prevention training covers how to recognize and avoid it.

Someone contacted me saying my crypto account is at risk. What do I do? +

Do not click any links or call any numbers they provide. This is almost certainly a phishing attempt. Legitimate exchanges and wallet providers will never contact you unsolicited to tell you your account is at risk. Go directly to the official website by typing the URL yourself, log in there, and check your account status. If you're unsure, call us at 1-239-990-1615 before doing anything.

Can I get my money back if I was scammed? +

Unfortunately, in most cases, no. Crypto transactions are irreversible. There are legitimate organizations that assist scam victims — including the FBI's Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3) and some state attorney general offices — but recovery is rare and never guaranteed. The best protection is prevention, which is why scam education is a core part of everything we do. If you believe you've been scammed, report it to the FBI at ic3.gov immediately.

Without proper planning, your crypto is gone — permanently. Over $300 billion in crypto has been permanently lost because of this. Our Complete Guardian package documents everything your family would need — step by step — so your digital wealth is never lost no matter what life brings.
